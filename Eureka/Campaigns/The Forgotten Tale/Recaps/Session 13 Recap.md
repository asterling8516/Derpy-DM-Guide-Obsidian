Last session, the ink on your contracts dried, and the true cost of employment was revealed.

Leaving the Marble Tower behind, you sought the quiet of your quarters aboard the _Happily Ever After_ airship. Adamson, thrilled by the rare luxury of a private room, left a trail of seedlings scattered across his floor. But rest was fleeting. A sudden warning echoed through the halls: a capsule launch to Mistveil was imminent. Moments later, the massive ship rocked violently as the payload was fired into the sky.

The next morning brought corporate reality. At eight A.M. sharp, you attended orientation. Elara inspected your newly implanted Mana Gems with a strange, discerning glass, ensuring the integration held. While she worked, Sleepy scoured the employee handbook, looking for loopholes.

Then came your directive. Your mission: travel to Frostlight, enter the Black Forest, and speak with "Mother." You are to open negotiations between _Happily Ever After_ and Ursula, ensuring the four capital cities never face an attack like the Giant Mantas again.

But Piper had questions of his own. Projecting footage of your secret excursion to the Marble Tower, he pressed you for answers. You kept your words vague, watching coldly as his drones and automatons swept the projected area on screen. Refusing to walk, you demanded transport to your mission, arguing that Piper had hired _you_, not your carriage.

Piper agreed. But the victory was a trap.

Aboard your new transport, Sleepy’s hostility toward the Captain grew. Though your questions yielded a terrifying revelation. Piper wasn't just a corporate entity. It was Fae. The Captain worked on a strict, renewable yearly contract, but your own terms had been left dangerously vague. The horrifying realization set in: you hadn't just taken a job. You had made a deal with a Fae, and you might be bound to _Happily Ever After_ for the rest of your lives.

True to a Fae's malicious compliance, Piper’s transport dropped you at the Spire of Frostlight—technically fulfilling the request, but leaving you miles from your actual destination.

You adapted. You rented a carriage pulled by two white Clydesdales, with Cinderella taking the reins and Adamson leading the way. But the frozen wastes are unforgiving.

A massive ambush shattered the journey. Five Frost Giants and their war mammoths descended upon you. In seconds, the carriage was reduced to splinters. Huge axes swung down, knocking heroes into the snow, unconscious and bleeding.

The carriage is gone. You are fighting for your lives on the frozen ground. Three Frost Giants and their mammoths still stand.

And that... is where we begin.
