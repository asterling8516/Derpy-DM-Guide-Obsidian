# Remastered
Last session, the road to Infernium was paved with stone and secrets.

You navigated a sea of petrified statues, carefully moving the frozen figures to clear a path. Ever the opportunist, Sleepy kept a sharp eye on the fleeing masses, noting the heavy purses of the wealthy. But as you pushed through the crowd, the curse finally began to break. Flesh replaced stone, and the petrified citizens awoke to the chaos around them.

Approaching Infernium, the scars of battle were fresh. A defeated Giant Manta lay beside the city, its massive corpse crawling with strange creatures. While some fought the swarm and others held a perimeter, you pushed inside the city walls.

Infernium was a hive of recovery. Employees of _Happily Ever After_ swarmed the streets—treating the wounded in medical tents, clearing poison clouds, neutralizing acid, and melting away the ice. Seeking a profit, you found a _Happily Ever After_ storefront to offload the artifacts you pulled from Tiamat's Cathedral. The dragonborn automaton behind the counter offered a staggering one hundred and fifty thousand gold. You played hardball, walking away to buy Sending scrolls instead.

Reaching out through the magic, you contacted Codex. His reply was cryptic: he was trapped inside a massive room filled with crates and boxes aboard some kind of ship, his exact location unknown.

Returning to the negotiating table, you finalized the deal with the dragonborn. In exchange for the artifacts, you secured not just the gold, but a promise of employment.

A transport ferried you through the city, finally docking at the massive _Happily Ever After_ airship. Here, the hospitality took a dark, mechanical turn. You were led to a sterile laboratory where massive gems were surgically embedded into your chests. You were then sealed inside fluid-filled test tubes—a necessary precaution, they claimed, to keep your bodies from exploding as the gems settled.

Once released, Piper told you to rest in your quarters on deck H-23 before orientation. When Sleepy demanded to know who was actually in charge, the strange robot locked eyes with him and delivered a chilling truth: "There is no higher power than I."

Free to roam, you took the chaotic, Wonka-style elevator down to the warehouse deck. Following Codex’s clues, you discovered the impossible: a floating island holding a pristine marble tower, hidden right inside the ship's hold.

Ascending the tower, you finally found Codex. You delivered the grim news, explaining that a terrifying hydra was responsible for the disappearance of the Glimfeather. As you described the beast, the pages of a nearby book magically began to turn, sketching the creature and recording your every word.

Codex sighed, admitting he would need to find a new librarian for Mistveil, and handed over your hundred thousand gold reward.

And that... is where we find ourselves today.
