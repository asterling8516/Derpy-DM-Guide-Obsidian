Last session, the skies offered a haunting glimpse into the past.

After a two-week staredown with Piper in Mistveil, your shopping finally concluded, and your true mission was set. You are to travel to the Black Forest and confront "Mother" regarding Ursula. You must find out why the Capital cities were attacked, and how to stop it from ever happening again.

The journey to the Spire in Frostlight took you directly over the Iron Meadow—ground zero for the Great War, five centuries past. Peering down from the ship, you spotted the impossible. Empty armor, wandering the cursed wastes. Figures walking in a graveyard where the living rarely return.

Arriving at the Spire, corporate business resumed. Silvia received her custom armor and prepared to begin her contract with _Happily Ever After_. But where the rest of you accepted the surgical implantation of Mana Gems, Silvia negotiated. She struck a hard bargain: Piper could study her, but no gem would pierce her chest, and all findings had to be kept strictly confidential. Piper agreed. No blood was spilled, but the measurements and questions were relentless.

Free for the moment, the party descended into the frozen streets of Frostlight. You discovered a shop called _The Tinkered Tail_, brimming with clockwork curiosities—including a remarkably crude imitation of Glimmer. Eager to skip the waitlist for custom automatons, Slicer took matters into his own hands... resulting in a rather mysterious, "fade-to-black" encounter in the backroom with the shop’s husband-and-wife owners.

Leaving with your new mechanical prizes, Fynn led the group to a grand church forged entirely from magically hardened ice, a sanctuary welcoming the worship of all pantheons. But inside, the divine felt fragile. Giant ice sculptures of the Gods loomed above, yet some were actively crumbling, defying the magic meant to hold them together. Silvia offered a chilling theory: the shattered statues belonged to dead gods, and the crumbling ones... were dying.

As Fynn carved a tribute to Lady Seraphina, Kookush caught a familiar, pungent scent.

He tracked it to a group of elves who were, quite literally, "high." They claimed they were worshipping the Fickle Fates by inhaling a volatile Arcane powder. Kookush immediately recognized the danger, warning that the highly addictive drug could cause the user to violently explode. Silvia was disgusted, immediately trying to figure out how to shut the dangerous practice down.

While the party interrogated the reluctant elves about their supply, Fynn decided to skip the diplomacy. Snatching a vial of the explosive powder, he bolted from the church. The elves merely watched in confusion, noting they would have just given it to him if he’d asked.

Unfurling her wings to a collective gasp of awe from the stoned worshippers, Silvia launched into the air, tackling Fynn in the snow to reclaim the dangerous drug.

But the chaos was not completely contained. In the aftermath, Slicer quietly took his second dose of the explosive Arcane powder... and he is growing quite fond of it.

And that... is where we find ourselves today.
