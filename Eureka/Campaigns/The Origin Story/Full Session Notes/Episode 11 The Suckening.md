# Echoes of the Lost and Crystal Fangs

## Act I: The Hall of Haunted Memories

The ruins grew colder as the party pushed further north, leaving behind the silent stone enclaves of the forgotten gods. Ahead lay a pair of translucent, shimmering "ghost doors"—the spectral remnants of barriers long since bypassed by ancient intruders. 

But as the party crossed the threshold, the air grew heavy, and a sudden, suffocating darkness snatched away their senses. A psychic shockwave ripped through their minds. Only Finn stood unshaken, his mind anchored against the mental assault, while the others collapsed or reeled, trapped in vivid, painful illusions of their pasts.

*   **Jersey’s Vision:** He found himself back in the rain-slicked ruins of his childhood home, but he was trapped inside his father’s battered, bloodied body. He relived the desperate moment his father made a pact with a drooling, acidic devil to protect young Jersey. In the shadows stood a silent observer—a finely dressed man twirling a golden pocket watch. Desperate to protect his son, Jersey's father begged this stranger for help. With a sinister smile, the watch-twirler agreed, promising Jersey protection until the end of his natural life, sealing the deal with a cold handshake.
*   **Finn’s Vision:** Though Finn remained conscious in the real world, a echo of his past tugged at his soul. He felt himself floating in a dark, endless void, listening to titanic voices arguing over his fate. One voice claimed him by right of blood; another, a burning, infernal voice, claimed his soul was already bought and paid for. He was violently pulled between a searing, hellish heat and a pure, radiant love, before waking with a thousand-yard stare.
*   **Naya’s Vision:** She was a child again, playing with mortal friends, when her wild fey magic surged out of control, blasting them with fatal frost. In her grief and panic, a voice offered her control in exchange for her freedom. Golden bracelets bound her wrists, and she was pulled into a brass lamp. The stranger with the golden pocket watch—*The Lost*—picked up her vessel with a smirk, promising to "put her power to good use."
*   **Garp’s Vision:** He relived a drunken night in his youth, stumbling through the streets of his old port town. A well-dressed man—whom he now recognized as *The Lost*—nudged him toward a tavern called the *Drunken Compass*. It was there that Garp would meet Coralli, setting his entire tragic destiny into motion. 
*   **Kakush’s Vision:** He was back in the great kingdom of Frostlight, facing false accusations of violating the Iron Law of magic. The faceless whispers and the paranoia of being hunted returned to him, accompanied by the maddening, rhythmic *tick-tick-tick* of a golden pocket watch spinning in the dark.

As the visions shattered, the psychic backlash dealt a devastating blow. Jersey and Garp collapsed, unconscious, while Kakush and Naya barely clung to life. Finn and Naya’s familiar, Glimmer, worked quickly to stabilize their dying companions, dragging them onto makeshift, petrified pelts that were as hard as solid rock.

## Act II: A Precarious Rest & The Skittering Swarm

Huddled in the damp stone chamber, the battered party attempted a desperate long rest. But peace is a luxury the ruins do not afford. 

During the first watch, a skittering sound echoed from the walls. A swarm of crystalline spiders—living, magic-reflecting gemstones—burst from the shadows.

*   **The Ambush:** Jersey, waking groggy and prone, immediately threw his quarterstaff like a javelin, lodging it firmly into a spider’s face—though he was now left weaponless. 
*   **The Mana Drain:** One of the larger spiders latched onto Finn, sinking its mandibles into his neck and draining his raw magic, leaving him severely exhausted. In a bizarre display of wild magic, Finn tried to "feed" the spider his mana, causing a chaotic surge that erupted into a harmless but highly distracting storm of bubbles.
*   **Naya's New Pet:** Rather than kill the spider clinging to her, Naya used her fey charm to soothe the beast. Feeding it a spell slot and shielding it with her *Armor of Agathys*, she successfully pacified the creature, naming it **Princess** and declaring it the party's new mascot—much to the horror of her companions.
*   **Shattering the Swarm:** Garp fired his revolver with deadly precision, blowing a spider off Finn's back, though the resulting arcane explosion dealt a painful psychic jolt to Finn. Kakush swung his *Stormerang*, shattering another beast, and harvested the valuable crystal remains, securing $150$ gold pieces worth of rare alchemical components.

## Act III: Shattered Gems and Secret Spoils

With the spiders defeated and the party fully rested, they began to search the chamber. 

In an old, ash-filled fire pit, Jersey discovered a heavy, iron-banded wooden tankard that refused to burn.

### Found Item: Iron-Banded Tankard
*   **Type:** Monk Weapon (Club) / Spell Focus
*   **Properties:** +1 to attack and damage rolls.
*   **Special Ability:** Has 3 charges. As a bonus action after hitting a creature, you can expend a charge to splash a blinding magical brew into their face (Con save or blinded until the next turn). Regains charges when filled with alcohol. It cannot be spilled unless the wielder intends to.

Moving down the northern hallway, Garp’s sharp eyes spotted a pair of raised pressure plates. To clear the path, Naya used her *Shape Water* cantrip to pour water into the cracks of the trap and freeze it, allowing Kakush to shatter the mechanism safely with a crowbar. 

Further in, they discovered a small alcove where two wooden chests were entirely encased in thick, growing purple crystals. 

*   **Jersey's Impatience:** Refusing to wait for a delicate solution, Jersey threw his crowbar aside and punched the first chest with his golden gloves. The chest—which contained several volatile alchemical grenades—exploded in his face, singeing Naya and severely damaging poor Princess the spider. 
*   **The Careful Approach:** For the second chest, Kakush and Naya used their freezing method to systematically crack the crystals away, carefully prying the container open to reveal three extraordinary treasures.

### Found Item: Diplomat's Mercy
*   **Type:** Pistol (+1)
*   **User:** Garp
*   **Properties:** This matte-black firearm with silver inlay can be collapsed into an unrecognizable metal frame (requiring a DC 20 Investigation check to identify as a weapon). Shots fired from this weapon are magically silenced beyond 10 feet.

### Found Item: Bio-Bolt Caster
*   **Type:** Light Crossbow (+1)
*   **User:** Kakush
*   **Properties:** Made of brass and glass, smelling of ozone and peppermint. Features a rotating chamber. As a bonus action, the wielder can load a vial of acid, alchemist's fire, or a healing potion into the chamber. It can be fired at allies to administer healing potions at range (dealing only 1 piercing damage).

### Found Item: Scimitar of the Four Winds
*   **Type:** Scimitar (+1)
*   **User:** Naya
*   **Properties:** Crafted from unmelting blue ice. If used as a spellcasting focus, it grants +1 to spell attacks. Once per turn, deals an extra 1d6 cold damage (matching Naya's Genie patron). Allows the wielder to cast *Levitate* on themselves once per day.

## Conclusion: The Pulsing Crossroad

The party retraced their steps and headed toward a major four-way intersection. As they walked, they passed strange, emerald-green crystals jutting from the stone. Unlike the purple gems, these pulsed with a rhythmic, organic heartbeat. 

A sudden, hypnotic wave washed over them, urging them to stay in the corridor forever and defend it against outsiders. Gritting their teeth, the adventurers fought off the mental compulsion, shaking their heads as they stepped into the center of the massive intersection.

Ahead of them lay three unexplored paths, and from the darkness to the north, the faint, unmistakable sound of heavy chains clanked against the stone...
