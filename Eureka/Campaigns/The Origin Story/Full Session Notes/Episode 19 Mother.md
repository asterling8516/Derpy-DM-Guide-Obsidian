# Campaign Recap: A Pact of Salt and Tea

## The Vault of the Iron Dwarf

The air in the subterranean depths was thick with tension and the smell of ozone. **Sylvia** had struck the massive vault door with her *Vorpal Scimitar*, but instead of cleaving the iron, the impact triggered a cascading series of defensive runes. Red warning lights flashed silently through the chamber, and the grinding of stone on stone echoed from the corridor behind them—the exit was closing. 

Realizing brute force would only bury them alive, the party turned to finesse. The vault door was flanked by a dial of numbered locks, many of which were decoy traps filled with centuries of dust. 

```
[Decoy Locks (Dust-Filled): 1, 3, 9]
```

**Kakush**, racking his brain for historical lore on the ancient human king, Theron, realized the key to the sequence lay in the fundamental constant of creation: the mathematical sequence of Pi ($3.14159...$). 
* By cross-referencing the sequence of Pi with the dust-filled decoys, the party calculated that the number **12** was the final, untouched lock in the sequence.
* **Garb** stepped forward with his thieves' tools. His first attempt met a magical feedback loop, momentarily wrenching his soul from his body to hover over the corpse of an iron dwarf on the floor. 
* Undeterred, and bolstered by **Tristol's** bardic inspiration, Garb tried again. With a masterful twist of his wrist, a heavy *click* echoed through the chamber. The vault door swung open.

## The Ascent of Lady Celeste

Inside the vault, resting upon a stone pedestal, was the prize: a glowing, liquid-like blue star orb. It radiated a profound, soothing light that instantly washed away the party's exhaustion. 

Before **Cutter** (Slicer's brother) could grab it, Sylvia snatched the orb and tossed it to **Slicer**, who handed it to his brother. With the vault's defenses still groaning, Sylvia deployed a ninja smoke bomb as a visual screen, and Tristol chanted the words of teleportation, pulling the party back to the safety of Cutter's camp.

After a bizarre and highly uncomfortable resting arrangement in the camp's hallway—which resulted in the collapse of Sylvia's wooden chair—the party awoke to find Cutter’s twelve elite companions gathering. 

The blue star was placed at the center of the tent. It flared with the brilliance of a miniature sun, dissolving the physical walls of the tent and transporting everyone into an endless fey expanse of swirling blue and pink clouds, populated by giant, glowing moths.

From the radiant mists descended **Lady Celeste**, an ethereal Elven Archfey clad in a dress woven from the clouds. 

```
                    * .  *  .
                 *  LADY CELESTE  . *
                .  (The Blue Fairy) .
                 * .  *  .  *  .
```

In gratitude for retrieving her focus, she offered the adventurers a fraction of her power. 
* **Sylvia** accepted the gift, receiving the **Boon of the Luminous Mind**.
* The others declined; Slicer chose to remain fiercely loyal to his jealous patron, while Garb and Kakush preferred not to anger their existing divine benefactors.

With the pact sealed, the dreamscape faded, depositing the party back into the material realm.

## Spoils of War & Brotherly Goodbyes

Before Cutter and his men departed for Shadows Haven, the brothers shared a quiet, emotional farewell. Cutter pulled Slicer into a fierce hug, promising that their paths would cross again.

With quiet restored to the camp, Kakush set about identifying the two legendary blades they had looted from the vault:

### 🗡️ Loot Identified
* **The Sword of Kas**  
  * *Weapon (Scimitar), Legendary (Requires Attunement)*  
  * A +3 sentient weapon harboring a dark, insatiable bloodlust. Deals an additional $2d10$ necrotic damage to undead. Grants the wielder the ability to cast *Call Lightning* and *Finger of Death*. 
  * *Note:* Kakush nearly succumbed to its murderous whispers upon touching it. Slicer wrapped it tightly in heavy cloth and stowed it deep in his pack as a "break-glass-in-case-of-emergency" weapon.
* **The Sword of Life Trapping**  
  * *Weapon (Any Sword), Rare (Requires Attunement)*  
  * On a critical hit, the target must succeed on a Charisma saving throw or be sucked into a pocket dimension within the blade. The blade currently holds 12 distinct reflections of captured souls.

## The Jealous Patron & The Black Forest

The party returned to the city of Frostlight to board their airship, charting a course for the mysterious **Black Forest** to find the entity known as **Ursula**. 

During the journey, Slicer attempted to mend relations with his sentient hexblade patron, who had been giving him the silent treatment. The sword, furious that Slicer had flirted with the power of the Blue Fairy, abruptly turned Slicer's body to solid stone mid-step. 

> *"You trust the dumbest man alive,"* Slicer pleaded internally, his intelligence of 4 working against him. *"I am your fool. I'm sorry."*

Sensing his genuine (if dim-witted) submission, the patron relented, restoring his flesh, though she remained chillingly quiet.

Three days later, the airship hovered over the Black Forest. The canopy was so dense that the forest floor was entirely obscured. Landing near an unnaturally crystal-blue lake at the forest's edge, the party approached the tree line. Instantly, thick, aggressive vines surged forward to block their path.

Sylvia produced a magical fey flower. As she spoke to it, the vines parted just enough for a single-file passage. Sylvia stepped through first—and the vines snapped shut behind her, trapping her alone in the dark.

## A Pact of Salt and Tea

Sylvia navigated the pitch-black, silent woods until she stumbled upon a secluded, two-story wooden cabin. Knocking on the door, she was greeted by a pale, silver-haired elven woman whose right side was heavily scarred by purple magical burns. She introduced herself simply as **Mother**.

After Sylvia demonstrated her peaceful intentions, Mother opened the forest's pathways to let the rest of the party join them. 

Inside the cozy cabin, Mother served the adventurers hot lavender tea and salted cookies. The party laid out their true purpose: they had been sent by the *Happily Ever After* syndicate to broker peace negotiations with Mother’s daughter, **Ursula**, who was responsible for the devastating attacks on the four surface cities.

Mother grew fiercely protective, claiming the surface dwellers had been cruel to her daughter. 

Stepping forward, Slicer channeled every ounce of his dramatic charisma. He looked Mother in the eyes and swore a solemn, binding oath: **"We guarantee Ursula's safety."**

Because fey magic governs the Black Forest, Slicer's words carried the weight of a binding contract. A warm, heavy energy settled in his chest, sealing the promise.

### 📜 The Terms of the Treaty
Mother agreed to the negotiations under three strict conditions:
1. **The Neutral Ground:** The meeting must take place within the Black Forest; she will not leave her domain.
2. **The Delegation:** *Happily Ever After* may send no more than three representatives.
3. **The Guarantor:** Slicer and his companions must be present at the meeting to personally enforce the guarantee of Ursula's safety.

---

## The Envoy of Steel

The party journeyed back to Frostlight to deliver the terms to their contact, **Piper**, who was speaking through one of the city's clockwork automatons. 

Upon hearing the terms and Slicer's binding oath, Piper agreed. However, the syndicate's idea of a "peaceful representative" was unconventional. From the spires of the city, a massive, fifteen-foot-tall, unarmed iron colossus boarded their airship. 

With the silent, towering iron giant in tow, the party set sail back toward the Black Forest, knowing that if a single drop of blood was spilled during the upcoming talks, Slicer's life would be forfeit to the magic of the fey.
